\begin{abstract}
We apply QLoRA to PaliGemma-3B on the RISC remote-sensing captioning dataset, reaching CIDEr 2.21 from a zero-shot baseline of 0.25. Two ablations extend the baseline recipe: a caption-selection strategy comparison, and a cumulative PEFT method comparison across vanilla LoRA, rsLoRA, LoRA+, and DoRA. A short exploration swaps the base to PaliGemma 2 with an identical recipe. The cumulative ablation indicates that recent PEFT improvements do not stack monotonically on this domain, and that rsLoRA scaling alone is the strongest single intervention at a fixed 500-step budget.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Vision-language models perform well at captioning but adapting them to specialized domains is expensive. QLoRA \cite{dettmers2023qlora} addresses this with 4-bit base-weight quantization plus low-rank adapters \cite{hu2022lora}. We apply it to PaliGemma-3B \cite{beyer2024paligemma}, a SigLIP+Gemma VLM, on the RISC dataset of 44{,}521 satellite images each with five captions. Contributions: (i) a fine-tuned model with CIDEr 2.21 (8.7$\times$ baseline), (ii) caption-selection ablation, (iii) cumulative PEFT method comparison, (iv) a brief PaliGemma 2 swap under the same recipe.

\section{Approach}

The dataset is split image-level (seed 42) into 37{,}843 train, 2{,}226 val, 4{,}452 test. Captions are stored as five separate fields; one is sampled per training step (the \emph{random} strategy). We use \texttt{google/paligemma-3b-pt-224}; base weights are 4-bit NF4 with double quantization (bf16 compute). LoRA adapters of rank 16 with $\alpha{=}32$ and dropout 0.05 attach to the seven attention and MLP projection modules, giving 22.6M trainable parameters (0.77\%). The main run adds rsLoRA scaling $\alpha/\sqrt{r}$ \cite{kalajdzievski2023rslora} and LoRA+ \cite{hayou2024loraplus} with a $16{\times}$ asymmetric LR on the B matrices. AdamW at $10^{-4}$, batch 4 with gradient accumulation 4, gradient checkpointing, 3\% warmup, 1 epoch (2{,}366 steps). Ablations share the recipe and cap at 500 steps. Metrics use pycocoevalcap (multi-reference, all five captions).

\textbf{Cumulative PEFT method ablation (Table~\ref{tab:peft}).} rsLoRA alone produces the highest CIDEr; the rsLoRA+LoRA+ stack trails rsLoRA alone; adding DoRA \cite{liu2024dora} recovers but does not surpass it. Two replicates of the same stack configuration (D and G) differ by 0.16 CIDEr, which we treat as an empirical inter-run variance floor; under this lens only rsLoRA marginally exceeds the noise band.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}

Two findings stand out. First, modern PEFT improvements do not stack monotonically on RISC at the 500-step budget; combined with the 0.16 CIDEr noise band from our replicates, vanilla LoRA is competitive when the learning rate is reasonable. Second, the base-model upgrade does not by itself buy quality on this domain: PG2 ties PG1 at a fixed budget, and the 1-epoch PG1 main run remains ahead of the 500-step PG2 run, so training duration outweighs the v1$\to$v2 choice. QLoRA delivered an 8.7$\times$ CIDEr gain with under 1\% of the model trainable. Future work: a full-epoch PG2 run, the instruction-tuned PG2 variant, and recent activation-driven initializations (EVA, CorDA) once their multimodal calibration paths stabilize.
